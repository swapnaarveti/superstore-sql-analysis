Superstore Sales Analysis (SQL Project)
About this project
I worked on this project to practice SQL using a retail dataset and to understand how businesses analyze sales and profit.
Along with basic queries, I also explored more advanced SQL concepts like CTEs and window functions to answer deeper business questions.
Tools used
PostgreSQL
DBeaver
SQL
Dataset
Superstore dataset with:
sales
profit
category and sub-category
region and city
discount
What I did
Basic analysis
Sales and profit by region
Category performance
Discount impact on profit
Advanced analysis
Used CTEs (WITH clause) to break down problems
Ranked top-performing products within each region
Grouped discount levels to understand their effect
Calculated profit margins for better business insight
Identified loss-making products and weak areas
Key insights
1. Regional performance
Some regions generate high sales but their profit margin is lower.
This shows that high sales do not always mean strong business performance.
2. Top products by region
Each region has different top-performing sub-categories.
This suggests customer demand varies by location.
3. Discount impact
Higher discounts often lead to lower average profit
In some cases, heavy discounts even lead to losses
This indicates discount strategies need better control.
4. Profit margin analysis
Some categories have strong sales but lower profit margins.
This may be due to higher costs or excessive discounting.
5. Loss-making products
A few sub-categories consistently generate negative profit.
These may need pricing changes or a different business strategy.
6. High sales but low profit
Some sub-categories generate high revenue but very little profit.
This is a concern because revenue looks strong, but actual earnings are weak.
7. Contribution analysis
Some categories contribute a large portion of total sales,
but they are not always the most profitable.
